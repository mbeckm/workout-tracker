# Workout Tracker Product Context

Product: Kinetic

Lane: Product app. Design serves repeated use, fast logging, progress feedback, confidence during a workout, and a small sense of delight every time a set is logged. Mobbin references point toward a blend of Hevy-style utility, Fitbod-style guided clarity, and Ladder-style approachable plan presentation.

Core job: Help people create strength training plans, log sets quickly, and understand whether they are getting stronger without making them wrestle with forms mid-session.

Primary users:
- Lifters who train 3-5 days per week and want a clean workout history.
- Lifters who want to see progress clearly: strength trends, recent bests, and whether their training is moving in the right direction.
- Busy people logging sets between exercises, often one-handed, sweaty, and distracted.

What the app should feel like:
- Fast, tactile, focused, and quietly premium.
- Fun and delightful in small moments, especially when logging a set or seeing progress.
- Motivating without hype.
- Light, clean, and calm enough for daily use, expressive enough to make progress feel visible.
- Native to iOS, with large touch targets, bottom-first controls, crisp hierarchy, and glanceable summaries.
- More like a calm strength tool than a fitness content feed: the next useful action should always be obvious.
- Serious enough for strength training, but friendlier and lighter than spreadsheet-first gym trackers.
- Like a luxury Apple-native tool: smooth, precise, tactile, and quietly beautiful rather than decorative.

Apple platform philosophy:
- Use Apple's toolkit as a product advantage, not an implementation afterthought.
- Adopt Liquid Glass for key surfaces where depth and smoothness help: bottom action bars, live logging controls, timer chips, history sheets, PR badges, and focused edit/reorder modes.
- Keep Liquid Glass restrained and functional. It should make the interface feel alive and premium without reducing legibility or turning every card into glass.
- Use haptic feedback as part of the workout loop: crisp confirmation when a set is saved, subtle ticks for steppers, a stronger but still tasteful pulse for PRs, and gentle feedback when timers complete.
- Motion should feel physical and continuous: set rows settle into done states, save actions compress and release, sheets glide up from the thumb zone, and glass surfaces morph smoothly during focused state changes.
- The product should feel like a precision instrument for training, with the tactility of a high-end Apple app and the restraint of a serious tool.

Voice:
- Direct, encouraging, and concise.
- Uses training language naturally: sets, reps, load, rest, PR, volume.
- Avoids influencer energy, gamified shouting, and guilt.

Anti-references:
- Busy fitness dashboards with everything competing for attention.
- Neon bodybuilding aesthetics.
- Purple/blue gradient SaaS gloss.
- Tiny spreadsheet-like logging fields.
- Generic wellness imagery that does not help the workout task.

First feature focus:
- Create a reusable workout plan: plan name, day label, exercise blocks, set targets, quick add, duplicate, reorder, and save.
- Log sets: choose an exercise from a plan, enter reps and weight quickly, mostly one-handed, with immediate progress feedback.
- Reuse previous values: remembered load, previous reps, last set, and recent best should reduce typing.
- Understand progress: see last performance, recent bests, estimated strength trend, and whether a lift is moving up.

Mobbin research implications:
- Borrow Hevy's exercise-block density for plan structure and set tables, but soften the spreadsheet feel with Kinetic's larger touch targets and clearer hierarchy.
- Borrow Fitbod's guided set-entry clarity and one-trend-first progress hierarchy.
- Borrow Ladder's friendly plan-card hierarchy so saved plans feel approachable and reusable.
- Borrow Nike Training Club's strong workout identity, but avoid turning Kinetic into a content-led fitness app.
- Borrow Strava's concise accomplishment framing while keeping Kinetic private and utility-first.
- Keep Kinetic distinct by staying light-first, quieter, and more direct than the darker or busier references.

Success criteria:
- A user can understand what to do next in under three seconds.
- Logging a set feels faster than writing it in Notes.
- Progress is clear enough that a user can tell whether they are getting stronger.
- The app feels rewarding to use without turning training into a noisy game.
- Empty plan, plan editing, set logging, progress, and personal-record states are designed from the start.
- A returning user can start from previous set values and complete a normal set log with one decisive tap.
- A user can tell why the app suggested a load or target because last session and recent best context are visible nearby.
